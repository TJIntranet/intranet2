16.   Rules and Norms: Bend, Break, or Follow? There are good and bad ways that rules and norms shape discovery and pursuit of passions. Laws and rules are just explicit norms. Therefore, if you are trying to discover pollution, the norm (law) against dumping toxic waste into your local river is – in fact – a very positive check on your discovery. On the other hand, some norms manifest as strong social pressures for or against certain pursuits by certain people. For many decades, American norms held that women suited only for household, clerical, or healthcare work. Thankfully, Ann Dunwoody, Ginni Rometty, Carol Greider, and many others are proving how stupid that norm was.  Instead of trying to apply a blanket approach to every rule, we will discuss (with cases) how you can apply critical, contextualized thinking to norms in order to understand which ones you should bend, break, and follow.
Presenter:  Ted Zagraniski
Room: Galileo Commons

33.   Nobody cares about your career....more than you: How to forge your own path in the corporate machine. There are a myriad of benefits to taking a job with a large established company straight out of school.  You can count on job stability, room to grow, a great benefits package, and easy assess to training opportunities.  What you don't count on is the anonymity - of being almost interchangeable with other entry level employees.  It's something that hit me hard initially, but also forced me to learn to be my own #1 advocate for my future and that when it comes to your career it's OK to be a little bit selfish. I learned how to turn menial grunt work tasks into opportunities to prove my worth and later as leverage when making requests to higher ups.  By being honest, open, up front and frankly, ballsy, I have successfully maneuvered my way through three distinctly different roles within the company. What did I learn? Not to feel guilty for leaving behind a project or project team when a new opportunity comes along and more importantly that construction isn't for me long term.  More importantly I learned how to move forward when a job isn't fulfilling and to keep trying until I end up doing something I truly love.  What's next for me? Leaving my job and moving to Europe for 2 years to earn a masters degree in Offshore Engineering from the Delft University of Technology in the Netherlands to jumpstart a career in offshore renewables.

34.   Running for Local Office (and why it matters) Heeding the call to local office may not seem as most highly sought-after or glorious of career moves, it is however vitally important to our everyday lives. That is why, as a graduate of FCPS (including TJ) and as a world language teacher, she decided to do something about it by running for the School Board in 2015.  At this presentation, you can hear about what led to this decision, what it takes to run for local office, and what you can do to get involved in local politics. Even though you may only hear about our state and national representatives, our local officials are working hard everyday to ensure that our county continues to thrive.
